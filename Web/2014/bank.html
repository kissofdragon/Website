Banking and Finance


Finance is often defined simply as the management of money. Modern finance, however, is a family of business activity that includes the origination, marketing, and management of cash and money surrogates through a variety of capital accounts, instruments, and markets created for transacting and trading assets, liabilities, and risks. Finance is both art and science.

Financial performance measures assess the efficiency and profitability of investments, the safety of debtors claims against assets, and the likelihood that derivative instruments will protect investors against a variety of market risks. Three overarching divisions exist within the academic discipline and practices of finance: personal, corporate and public finance. Financial risk management is the practice of creating and protecting economic value in a firm. Financial economics concentrates on influences of real economic variables on financial ones. Financial mathematics is a field of applied mathematics, concerned with financial markets.Experimental finance aims to establish different market settings and environments to observe experimentally agents' behaviour. Behavioural finance studies how the psychology of investors or managers affects financial decisions and markets. Banks are institutions which facilitate transfer of funds from those who have it to those who do not in a way which leads to creation of wealth in the most efficient manner. Loans have become increasingly packaged for resale. Financial assets, known as investments, are financially managed with careful attention to financial risk management to control financial risk. Central banksare strong players in public finance, acting as lenders of last resort as well as strong influences on monetary and credit conditions in the economy
